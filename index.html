ARISTA Member Applications

If you are interested in bettering your community, both within Stuyvesant and throughout NYC, then we highly encourage you to apply. Our members uphold the four pillars of scholarship, character, service, and leadership by volunteering with organizations across the city, leading Office Hours sessions, providing one-on-one peer tutoring, and participating in our 3 committees, among many other opportunities. Although it is a significant commitment, ARISTA members are an important part of the school community, and gain invaluable experience as leaders.

While ARISTA members have typically consisted of motivated juniors and seniors, we created the brand-new Sophomore Program to give underclassmen a chance to complete service and gain mentorship from upperclassmen ARISTA members. Members of the Sophomore Program have significantly reduced credit requirements.

All freshmen, sophomores, and juniors with an overall GPA of 92 and above are welcome to apply by creating an account at apply.stuyarista.org. You will be required to write three short essays, which will be used to consider your application along with your activities and community engagement inside and outside of Stuyvesant. Applications are due on Monday, April 12th at 11:59pm.

Applications will be reviewed anonymously by the Executive Council and our Faculty Advisor, Mr. Wisotsky, with decisions released in early May. If you have any other questions, please feel free to email us at stuyaristanyc@gmail.com and check out our website at stuyarista.org.

We look forward to reading your applications! Best, ARISTA EC

Brooklyn Tech x Stuy Speaker Series!

The Freshman Caucus is excited to kick off our Speaker Series, a series of professionals that will be invited to share interesting information about their craft. Our first speaker is Dr. David Deitcher, a Cornell neuroscience professor. He will be doing a presentation about seizures and epilepsy, including the “julius seizure” gene he helped discover.

Ms. Maggio, Ms. Fang, and Mrs. Garber will be offering extra credit for the students that attend! If you want to receive extra credit for her class, you must come to the event, take notes on what you heard, and then submit the notes through the exit survey we will provide. - FC External Outreach Team
